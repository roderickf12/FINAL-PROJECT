By DAndre Ball
July 22, 2021 Print On a recent day at The Grill Studios in Emeryville, LOE Gino is talking about his upbringing in West Berkeley and his love for Seattle.
A few moments into the conversation, while reflecting on his childhood, the rapper
cuts himself off mid-sentence and says,
“Blood really didn’t pick my family. It was just circumstances.”
It is just this type of personal reflection that has helped LOE Gino develop a loyal fanbase throughout the Bay Area and Pacific Northwest.
He gives his fans the opportunity to get to know
him as an artist through lyrical reflections that touch on topics such as
family loss, fatherhood and Black identity.
